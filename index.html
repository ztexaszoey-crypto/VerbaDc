Real example from chemistry (what students actually struggle with)

You asked Verba:

How many grams of NaCl are needed to make a 2.0 M solution in 500 mL of water?

Verba's response:

  1. 1. Convert 500 mL to liters: 500 mL = 0.5 L
  2. 2. Moles of NaCl needed = Molarity × Volume = 2.0 × 0.5 = 1.0 mol
  3. 3. Molar mass of NaCl = 22.99 (Na) + 35.45 (Cl) = 58.44 g/mol
  4. 4. Mass = moles × molar mass = 1.0 × 58.44 = 58.44 grams
Verba always explains why each step matters — so you can solve the next one yourself.
