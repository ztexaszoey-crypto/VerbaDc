A real AP Chemistry problem and how Verba explains it.

Your question

How many grams of NaCl are needed to make a 2.0 M solution in 500 mL of water?

Verba explains:

  1. 1. Molarity = moles of solute ÷ liters of solution
  2. 2. Volume = 500 mL = 0.5 L → moles = 2.0 × 0.5 = 1.0 mol
  3. 3. Molar mass of NaCl ≈ 58.5 g/mol
  4. 4. Mass = 1.0 mol × 58.5 g/mol = 58.5 grams
